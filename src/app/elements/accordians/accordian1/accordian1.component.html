What is CPR?

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) is a lifesaving technique used in emergencies when a person's breathing or heartbeat has stopped.

Who should perform CPR?

Anyone trained in CPR can perform it. In emergencies, even untrained individuals can perform hands-only CPR.

How deep should chest compressions be?

At least 2 inches deep for adults.

How fast should chest compressions be?

100-120 compressions per minute.

When should I stop CPR?

When professional help arrives, the victim regains consciousness, or you are physically unable to continue.

Should I perform mouth-to-mouth CPR on everyone?

No, hands-only CPR is effective for adults. Mouth-to-mouth is optional and primarily recommended for children and drowning victims.

Do I need certification to perform CPR?

Can CPR restart a heart?

No, but it keeps blood and oxygen circulating until professional help arrives.

Can an AED be used on children?

Yes, but use pediatric pads if available.
